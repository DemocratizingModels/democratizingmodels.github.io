# Description of the Work Plan

The success of the DEMOS initiative hinges on addressing **three key pillars** that collectively enable the seamless sharing, use, and evolution of models across disciplines.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Universal Model Standard

Development of an universal model standard, referred to as **DEMOS standard**, will ensure that even the most complex models can be serialized, shared, and reused effectively, bridging the gaps between diverse research fields and software ecosystems.

[View Pillar 1 details →](pillar1/index.md)

### 2. Digital Infrastructure

A robust digital infrastructure is essential to facilitate the discovery, validation, and integration of models, enabling researchers to interact with them through accessible portals and tools.

[View Pillar 2 details →](pillar2/index.md)

### 3. Community Engagement

Active community engagement is critical to fostering adoption, ensuring the format and infrastructure meet user needs, and cultivating a sustainable ecosystem of collaboration and innovation.

[View Pillar 3 details →](pillar3/index.md)

## Deliverables

Throughout the documentation, deliverables are flagged for internal tracking purposes. Key deliverables include:

### Technical Deliverables

- DEMOS standard specification (WP1.1)
- Computational engines in three languages (WP1.2)
- MaaS protocol and implementation (WP1.3)
- Model registry and portal (WP2.1, WP2.2)
- Integration tools for existing repositories (WP2.3)

### Community Deliverables

- Documentation and tutorials (WP3.1)
- Showcase model collection (WP3.2)
- White paper and PDG articles (WP3.1)
- Community workshops and training (WP3.1)
- Governance structure (WP3.3)
