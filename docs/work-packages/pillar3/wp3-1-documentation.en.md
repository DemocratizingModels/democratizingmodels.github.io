# WP 3.1: Documentation and Outreach

## Overview

The first task of Pillar 3 focuses on creating accessible and comprehensive documentation and educational resources to lower the entry barriers for diverse user groups, for both developers and non-developers. This work package is essential for ensuring widespread adoption and understanding of the DEMOS standard across the ErUM community.

## Objectives

- Create comprehensive, accessible documentation for all user levels
- Develop engaging educational resources and multimedia content
- Organize regular workshops, hackathons, and training sessions
- Integrate DEMOS standard into educational curricula and outreach programs
- Ensure the standard is featured in authoritative physics publications
- Build a diverse and inclusive community through targeted outreach

## Documentation Resources

### User Manuals and Guides

Comprehensive user manuals, FAQs, and best-practice guidelines will be developed and made available through the portal. These materials will:

- Serve both new and experienced users
- Cover all aspects of model creation, serialization, and sharing
- Provide troubleshooting guides and common pitfalls
- Include code examples and templates
- Be maintained and updated with community feedback

### Multimedia Resources

To accommodate different learning styles and ensure accessibility, we will create:

- **Captioned video tutorials:** Step-by-step guides for common tasks
- **Webinars:** Live sessions with Q&A for real-time interaction
- **Interactive examples:** Hands-on demonstrations using the portal
- **Blog posts:** In-depth discussions of features and use cases
- **Podcasts:** Interviews with developers and domain experts

**Target:** 10 educational resources by 2028 (4 videos, 4 blog posts, 2 podcasts)

## Workshops and Training Events

### Community Workshops

Annual large-scale workshops bringing together the ErUM community:

- **Duration:** 2 days
- **Participants:** ~50 attendees
- **Frequency:** 2026, 2027, 2028
- **Content:**
  - Hands-on experience with DEMOS standard and portal
  - Presentations of showcase models from different domains
  - Discussion of community needs and standard evolution
  - Networking opportunities across disciplines

**First Workshop (2026.Q1):** Review and finalize the design and first-priority domain-specific components

### Domain-Specific Mini-Workshops

Focused training sessions for specific research domains:

- **Duration:** 1 day
- **Participants:** ~20 attendees
- **Frequency:** At least 3 per year (2026-2028)
- **Domains:** Hadron physics, particle physics, nuclear physics, neutrino physics, astroparticle physics, laser physics
- **Content:**
  - Domain-specific model features and primitives
  - Use cases and best practices
  - Integration with existing workflows
  - Hands-on serialization exercises

### Hackathons

Intensive collaborative coding events:

- **Planned:** 2 hackathons (2025, 2026)
- **Focus:**
  - Rapid prototyping of model serializations
  - Development of conversion tools
  - Integration with existing frameworks
  - Community-driven feature development

### Learning Sessions in Large Collaborations

Integration of DEMOS training into existing collaboration meetings:

- **Target:** At least 4 sessions by 2027.Q4
- **Venues:**
  - Belle II (LMU coordination)
  - LHCb (RUB-H coordination)
  - BES III (JGU coordination)
  - ATLAS (TUDO coordination)
- **Format:** Tutorial sessions during collaboration weeks

## Publications and Academic Integration

### White Paper

A comprehensive white paper (2028.Q3) will provide:

- Detailed explanations of the DEMOS standard
- Concrete examples from all represented domains
- Best practices for model serialization
- Technical specifications and validation procedures
- Vision for future developments

### Particle Data Group (PDG) Articles

**RUB-H**, as an active PDG member, will ensure DEMOS standard visibility:

- **PDG Review Articles (2028.Q1):** Feature DEMOS standard in existing reviews
- **New PDG Review Article:** Advocate for a dedicated article on modeling practices and model sharing, addressing emerging needs for standardized computational research approaches

These PDG publications will provide authoritative documentation and establish DEMOS as a recognized standard in the physics community.

### Domain-Specific Publications

Model standardization (WP 1.1) will culminate in field-specific publications detailing:

- Standardization of terminology, conventions, and notation
- Best practices for each domain
- Common primitives and building blocks
- Integration examples with domain-specific tools

### Masterclass for Pupils

**Led by:** JGU and RUB-H
**Target Audience:** High school students
**Timeline:** 2028.Q1

The masterclass on hadron physics will:

- Highlight potential applications of the DEMOS project
- Use models from the DEMOS registry to interpret experimental data
- Provide interactive exploration of physics phenomena
- Demonstrate how students can engage with cutting-edge research

## Accessibility and Inclusivity

All documentation and outreach materials will:

- Be available in multiple formats (text, video, audio)
- Include captions and transcripts for accessibility
- Use clear, jargon-free language where possible
- Provide glossaries for technical terms
- Be available through the open portal

Workshops and events will:

- Actively recruit diverse participants
- Provide financial support where needed
- Offer virtual participation options
- Create inclusive, welcoming environments

## Continuous Feedback and Improvement

The documentation and outreach strategy will evolve based on:

- **User feedback surveys** (2027.Q3 onwards): Regular surveys distributed via mailing lists to gauge user satisfaction and identify areas for improvement
- **Community interaction metrics:** Track engagement with materials and events
- **Adoption indicators:** Monitor usage of standard across collaborations
- **Educational impact assessment:** Evaluate effectiveness of outreach programs

## Success Indicators

By the end of the project (2028.Q3), we expect:

1. **Documentation Coverage:** Complete documentation for all standard features and engines
2. **Event Participation:** 150+ researchers engaged through workshops and training
3. **Educational Resources:** 10 high-quality multimedia resources accessible to diverse audiences
4. **Collaboration Integration:** DEMOS training integrated into at least 4 major collaboration workflows
5. **Publication Impact:** White paper published and PDG articles featuring DEMOS
6. **Educational Outreach:** Successful masterclass and research school with positive feedback
7. **Community Growth:** Active mailing list and communication channels with regular engagement
